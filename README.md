## Project Overview

This project delivers an **end-to-end HSQE analytics dashboard** designed to provide leadership with a unified view of **Health, Safety, Quality, and Environmental performance**.

The solution integrates incident, compliance, workforce, and sustainability data into a **single decision-support system**, enabling proactive risk management and operational improvement.

---

## Business Context

HSQE data is often fragmented across multiple systems:

- Incident reporting tools  
- Audit systems  
- Training records  
- Equipment compliance logs  
- Environmental datasets  

This creates limited visibility and prevents leadership from answering:

- Where are risks concentrated?  
- What is driving incidents?  
- Are compliance gaps increasing exposure?  
- How does operational activity impact safety and sustainability?  

---

## Problem Statement

The organisation lacked a **centralised analytics framework** to:

- Track incidents and severity trends  
- Link compliance metrics to risk exposure  
- Identify high-risk sites and departments  
- Measure operational impact (lost time)  
- Monitor sustainability alongside safety  

As a result, decisions were reactive rather than data-driven.

---

## Project Objectives

- Provide a **real-time HSQE performance view**
- Identify **risk drivers and root causes**
- Monitor **compliance and workforce readiness**
- Track **lost time and operational disruption**
- Analyse **environmental impact across sites**
- Enable **data-driven decision making**

---

## Tools Used

- Power BI (Dashboard & Visualisation)  
- DAX (KPI Measures & Calculations)  
- Excel / CSV (Data Preparation)  
- Data Modelling (Relational Structure)

---

## Dashboard Structure

The dashboard is structured into four key views:

1. Executive Performance Overview  
2. Incident, Risk & Compliance Analysis  
3. Compliance & Workforce Readiness  
4. Sustainability & Operational Impact  

---

## Executive Summary

- **300 Total Incidents**
- **39 High Severity Incidents**
- **30 RIDDOR Cases**
- **1,000 Lost Time Days**
- **79.6 Average Audit Score**
- **79% Training Completion**
- **83% Equipment Compliance**

### Key Takeaways

- Risk is **distributed but uneven**
- A few areas drive **disproportionate impact**
- Compliance alone does not guarantee safety
- Lost time is a **major operational cost driver**

## Key Insights

- Near misses represent **early warning signals**

---

## Recommendations

### 1. Site-Level Strategy
- Manchester → Reduce incident volume & RIDDOR exposure  
- Birmingham → Focus on severity reduction  
- Leicester → Improve training completion  
- London → Maintain and replicate best practices  

### 2. Address Root Causes
- Improve equipment maintenance processes  
- Strengthen workforce training programs  
- Standardise operational procedures  

### 3. Improve Workforce Readiness
- Increase training completion to **95%+**  

### 4. Strengthen Equipment Compliance
- Reduce non-compliance below **5%**

### 5. Leverage Near Miss Data
- Use near misses as **predictive risk indicators**

### 6. Align Sustainability with Operations
- Focus on high-energy, high-incident locations
